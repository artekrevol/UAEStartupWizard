# Anti-Money Laundering (AML) Guidelines for DMCC Companies

## Introduction
All DMCC member companies must establish adequate AML procedures to prevent, detect, and report money laundering activities in accordance with UAE Federal laws and DMCC regulations.

## Key AML Requirements

### 1. Risk Assessment
- Conduct a business-wide money laundering risk assessment
- Identify and assess customers based on risk profiles
- Regular review and update of risk assessments
- Documentation of risk assessment methodology and findings

### 2. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)
- Verification of customer identity using reliable sources
- Identification of beneficial owners
- Understanding the purpose and nature of business relationships
- Enhanced due diligence for higher-risk customers
- Simplified due diligence for lower-risk customers

### 3. Ongoing Monitoring
- Regular review of customer transactions and activities
- Update of customer information as needed
- Transaction monitoring systems implementation
- Red flags identification and escalation procedures
- Periodic review of high-risk customers

### 4. Record Keeping
- Maintain records for at least 5 years
- Documentation of CDD information and transaction history
- Training records of employees
- Documentation of suspicious activity reports
- Evidence of compliance with AML obligations

### 5. Suspicious Transaction Reporting
- Procedures for internal reporting of suspicious activities
- Assessment of suspicious activity reports
- Filing Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) with the UAE FIU
- Confidentiality of reporting process
- Non-tipping off provisions

### 6. AML Training
- Initial AML training for all employees
- Regular refresher training
- Specialized training for compliance officers
- Training documentation and assessment
- Updates on new typologies and red flags

### 7. AML Compliance Program
- Appointment of a Money Laundering Reporting Officer (MLRO)
- Development of written AML policies and procedures
- Regular independent review and testing
- Management reporting on AML compliance
- Remediation of identified compliance gaps

## Common AML Red Flags
- Transactions inconsistent with customer's business profile
- Complex or unusual transaction patterns
- Reluctance to provide identity information
- Transactions with high-risk jurisdictions
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Shell companies with no apparent business purpose
- Unexplained sources of funds

## Penalties for Non-Compliance
Non-compliance with AML regulations can result in:
- Substantial financial penalties
- Criminal prosecution
- License suspension or revocation
- Reputational damage
- Restrictions on banking services

For AML compliance assistance, please contact the DMCC Compliance Department.
